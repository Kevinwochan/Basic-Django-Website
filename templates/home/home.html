FinHack

Australia has the potential to be a future global leader in fintech - that’s why Textbook Ventures and Reinventure Group have joined forces to launch the first student fintech hackathon “FinHack” to give uni students an opportunity to hack the future of fintech.

Students will problem-solve with, and be mentored by, some of Australia’s most promising fintech companies to understand the unique opportunities offered by Australia's fintech industry. Students from all universities, faculties, and experiences are welcome to take part!

The event is sponsored by Reinventure Group, Westpac’s VC arm, to help students take their first step into a booming industry and to encourage great ideas to bloom in Australian fintech.

Sponsors

Reinventure is pioneering Founder-First Corporate Venture Capital. They champion founders who have the vision, ambition and hustle to transform an industry across Asia-Pacific and beyond, propelling them with the support of a top 20 bank globally.

The Challenge

A real-estate platform that allows consumers to buy and sell very small stakes in residential properties.

A online platform aiming to help SMEs secure loans, matching users with lending partners through an algorithm.

A app cutting consumer's wait time for their everyday purchases whilst enabling merchants to manage their peak loads.

Three of the best fintech startups in Reinventure’s portfolio have come up with three “Challenges”, opportunities to change the fintech landscape. Teams will choose one of these challenges to solve in 24 hours.

Each problem is intended to be broad and teams are encouraged to use their creative juices to find the best solution to the problem.

Each fintech startup out of the total three from Reinventure’s portfolio will manage a problem scenario and teams will have the opportunity to be mentored by these startups to get to the nitty gritty of the problem.

Criteria

Teams are judged on four qualities:

1.Relevance

Does the solution adequately solve the challenge presented? Could it be leveraged by the venture seamlessly?

2.Creativity

Has the team used all their creative juices to arrive at the solution and its application/execution?

3.Sophistication

Is the solution technically sophisticated? Can the technical solution be easily replicated or does it show a level of complexity?

4.Polish

Is the solution workable with a smooth user experience? Is there a high level of attention to detail in the design?

FAQ

Who can compete at FinHack?

FinHack is open to any student 18 years and older, enrolled in a university, this includes postgraduate studies. We reserve the right to cancel your ticket if you do not meet this requirement.

How many people can be in a team?

Teams can have up to 6 people. If you apply by yourself or with less than 6, we will assign team members to complete your team's skillset.

Is the hackathon just about Fintech?

Definitely not! Although the 3 mentoring startups are all fintechs, the solution to each “Challenge” doesn’t need to be a fintech solution and we strongly encourage students to draw on their experiences from outside of finance and tech.

What if I don’t study finance?

FinHack is open to students of all disciplines so you don’t have to study finance! You just need to have a creative & open mind and a thirst for hacking!

What’s in it for me?

Aside from meeting 3 of Australia’s top fintechs, and rubbing shoulders with partners from Reinventure Group, students will have the opportunity to hack away at big challenges facing the fintech industry in teams of like-minded and motivated students.

Can we work on our idea beforehand?

The problem scenarios will only be released on the night of the hack’s launch (Friday, 10th August). Generally participants are not allowed to start any development or design work on your project before the hack especially code, graphics, visuals and 3D designs.

Students are encouraged however to research the fintech space and the three startups from Reinventure’s portfolio.

Can we work on existing projects?

No, you will not be allowed to work on existing projects at FinHack. However you can synthesis previous ideas, but not tangible products.

Do I have to stay overnight?

You are not required to stay overnight. The venue will be closed each night at 6pm and will open again at 7am the next day. You may work on the project at home.

I have no hackathon experience can I still take part?

Of course! We want to encourage as many students to take part as possible. The problem scenarios are designed to be broad to allow participants to use their creativity.

What’s more, we will run short workshops and mentoring sessions throughout to support you as much as possible.

Where can I find people to form a team?

The best way to find a team is to join the FinHack Facebook Group. There will also be time allotted on the Friday night opening event to meet others and form teams.

What should I bring?

A laptop, mobile phone, chargers, and your shiniest thinking caps.

Is there a Code of Conduct?

Yes, there is a code of conduct. FinHack follows "The Hack Code of Conduct". This applies to all attendees, sponsors, partners, volunteers and staff. You can view the code of conduct here.

Who owns the solution after the hack?

The solution remains the property of the team members and the teams during and after the hack.
